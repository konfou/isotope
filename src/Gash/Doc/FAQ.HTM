Gash FAQ

Q.What is Gash?
A. A programming language.  It also comes with an integrated development environment complete with debugger, etc.

Q. What the @#$! do we need another language for?
A. Well, C++ is a good powerful language, but its difficult to crank out high-quality apps in C++ quickly.  You could switch to one of those other languages, but then you'd lose the power of C++.  Gash lets you develop apps in an easy language without making you leave C++.

Q. What do you mean by "you don't have to leave C++ to use Gash"?
A. Gash can call your C++ methods and C++ can call your Gash methods.  You can crank out the apps quickly in Gash and still write the functions you want in C++.

Q. What will it cost me?
A. Nothing.  Gash is free.  And you can do whatever you want with the programs you develop with Gash.

Q. Do I have to link some library to my C++ app?
A. Yes.

Q. What license is Gash distributed by?
A. The LGPL

Q. Does Gash do garbage collection?
A. Yes.  Of course.  I said it was an easy language.

Q. Do I need to know C++ to use Gash?
A. No.  You can make apps in just Gash if you want.

Q. Do you want me to switch to Gash?
A. "switching" is not necessary.  Use whatever platform you want.  Gash is just a tool to make your life easier.  If you ever decide you don't like it anymore, it will compile back to C++ souce code so you can walk away unscathed.

Q. Does Gash run in a Virtual Machine or does it compile to x86 instructions?
A. Whichever you want.  The debugger uses the virtual machine.  The virtual machine is also there in case you want to run apps off the net in a guaranteed safe environment.  Compiled x86 apps, however, will run very fast.

Q. How easy is it to develop Gash apps?
A. The Gash IDE is 100% menu-based (not editor-based).  That means you never have to just know what to type.  Your choices are always before you.  It will take you a while to get used to--any new language would.  But you will find out that Gash is as easy as they get.

Q. Can I still edit the source in a text editor?
A. Yes.  The source is XML-based.
